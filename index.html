A Note on Method
Suppose a fair coin is tossed.
The coin is not tossed.
The scenario is constructed. The mathematics is what it is.
Statistics is taught through invented scenarios — dice, urns, card games. Econometrics assumes people are rational, their commonalities are linear and their eccentricities follow instructions. Meanwhile, somewhere in the background, a latent variable crosses an unseen threshold. What remains implicit throughout is that this is already world-building — with rules, inhabitants, and an internal logic. This project makes that acknowledgement explicit.

Each world takes place in a fictional setting whose internal logic is the mathematics itself — the story arc mirrors the syllabus, the characters embody the concepts, and the gamification is built into the world rather than layered on top of it.

Each concept is presented through multiple lenses — intuitive, algebraic, applied — and students move through the material at their own pace, choosing their own path through the world. At each stage of exploration, an AI tutor enters the conversation having already read the student's full engagement history: which rooms were visited, which were skipped, where errors were filed. What follows is a genuinely personalised Socratic dialogue — not a generic exchange, but one shaped by exactly what that student has and has not yet understood.
