DEAL The cards have been dealt. I am cautiously optimistic. The probability of a natural blackjack is 4.83\%. I note this. A standard 52-card deck. I have memorised the probabilities. I have studied basic strategy. I have also ignored some of it. The sample space has been dealt. I am ready. I remain, by any reasonable measure, prepared. Cards distributed from a uniform distribution. Theoretically. BUST Bust. My hand value exceeded 21. This is, I have learned, bad. The probability of busting was calculable. I chose not to calculate it. I have exceeded 21. This was not one of my aims. Bust. The card I requested was not the card I required. I should have stood. Now I sit with the outcome. An unfavourable outcome. I am updating my strategy. WIN I have won. The house edge is real but so, apparently, is variance. The dealer busted. This is statistically satisfying. I win. This outcome has probability greater than zero. Variance in my favour. I will take it. A win. I am not surprised. I am slightly surprised. The theory holds. For now. Odds well that ends well. LOSE Even with optimal play, the house edge is approximately 0.5\% . The chips are down. My chips are down. This feels like a raw deal. Riches to ruins.
