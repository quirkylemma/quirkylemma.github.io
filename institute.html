The Institute  □ Ground Floor

The lights of the casino are on.

On the other side of the road, in relative darkness, stands the Institute. It is a building of uncertain provenance, constructed during the 1980s. Its style suggests the architect had strong feelings about concrete. It houses ten floors.

Candidate Squeakwell stands on the ground floor of the Institute. He is there to file his application for entry to the casino. The requisite framework for entry is mathematical. It is available on the floors above. The floors of the Institute are to be visited in order. Or not.

The casino is called Gambler's Ruin. Its lights are always on. Several applications have been filed at the Institute. None have been successful yet.
